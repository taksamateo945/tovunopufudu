    AKA’s Music Career

    AKA, whose real name was Kiernan Jarryd Forbes, was born on January 28, 1988, in Cape Town, South Africa.

    He started his music career as a member of a group called Entity, which disbanded in 2006.

    AKA then went solo and released his debut album, Altar Ego, in 2011.

    The album was a hit, with singles like “Victory Lap” and “I Want It All” topping the charts.

    AKA’s second album, Levels, was released in 2014 and was certified platinum by the Recording Industry of South Africa (RISA).

    The album featured collaborations with artists like Burna Boy, Da L.E.S, and JR.

    Some of the popular songs from the album were “Congratulate”, “Run Jozi”, and “All Eyes on Me”.

    AKA’s third album, Touch My Blood, was released in 2018 and was also certified platinum by RISA.

    The album had singles like “Fela in Versace”, “Beyonce”, and “Jika”.

    AKA also released two EPs, Bhovamania (2020) and Today’s Tragedy, Tomorrow’s Memory (2021), before his death.

    AKA won several awards for his music, including Best Male Artist at the South African Music Awards (SAMA), Best Hip Hop Album at the Metro FM Music Awards, and Best Collaboration at the MTV Africa Music Awards.

    He was also nominated for the BET Awards and the MTV Europe Music Awards.

    AKA’s Other Sources of Income

    Besides music, AKA had other sources of income that contributed to his net worth.

    He had endorsement deals with brands like Reebok, Cruz Vodka, Huawei, and Betway.

    He also had his own app called Beam Digital, which allowed fans to stream his music and access exclusive content.

    AKA also ventured into other businesses, such as owning a restaurant called Cuba BLK in Durban, and launching his own TV channel called AKA TV on Vodacom’s Video Play platform.

    He also had plans to launch his own sneaker line called SneAKA.

    AKA’s Assets and Expenses

    AKA lived a lavish lifestyle and spent his money on expensive cars, houses, and jewelry.

    He owned a:

  • BMW i8 worth R2 million,
  • Mercedes-Benz G-Wagon worth R3 million,
  • Range Rover Sport worth R2.5 million

    • He also bought himself a flashy house in 2016 when he and DJ Zinhle were expecting their first child together.

    AKA also loved to travel and perform around the world.

    He visited countries like Nigeria, Ghana, Kenya, Tanzania, Mozambique, Zimbabwe, Botswana, Namibia, Angola, and more.

    AKA also supported various causes and charities.

    He donated R100 000 to the Wits SRC Humanitarian Fund in 2016 to help students with financial difficulties.

    He also participated in the #FeesMustFall campaign to protest against high tuition fees in South African universities.

    AKA also donated R20 000 to the Nelson Mandela Children’s Hospital in 2017.
